Before there was a fleet, there was Poppa.

Bob Epstein wrote about Florida Keys fishing for the Miami Herald for decades. Photojournalist, author of more than a dozen books, and a fixture of the Islamorada docks, he fished with George H. W. Bush and Ted Williams and put the Keys bite into print for generations of anglers. Everyone called him Poppa.

When he passed in January 2025, his sons David and Brian Epstein did the only thing that made sense: they built a charter operation worthy of his name. The Poppa Wahoo Collective runs four boats out of Islamorada, crewed by captains who were mentored by names like Stu Apte, Flip Pallot, and Lefty Kreh. It is veteran owned, family run, and serious about one thing above all: sending you home with a story Poppa would have written up.
